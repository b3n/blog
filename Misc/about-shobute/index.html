Shobute ☘

Misc / About Shobute

Shobute is the blog of Ben, a computer science student from England.

The word “shobute” comes from the Japanese 勝負手, which is an all-or-nothing move in the game Go. It’s the kind of risky play you have to make if you start to fall behind.

This blog is not inteded as an online diary, I will not be posting what I had for breakfast; instead I will post things which others (likely coming from Google) may find useful. There’s no specific topic, posts may be about anything. The design is intentionally minimalistic, I want the focus to be the content.

Everything posted, unless explicitly stated in the post itself, is in the public domain. You do not have to ask permission to copy/use/modify anything, you don’t even have to attribute me. You can rip-off all my work and stick ads on it for all I care, it doesn’t bother me in the slighest. Do what you like with it.

Unlike traditional blogs, posts on Shobute are not static, they are all considered a work-in-progress and thus may be modified or deleted at whim and without notice. There’s no publish dates, only the latest update dates, which is what posts are sorted by. If you want to link to or reference a specific version of a post I suggest you upload it elsewhere.

If you use a news-reader there’s an Atom feed at /feed. If your web browser does not support Unicode or modern HTML (cough IE cough) you’re going to have a bad time.
